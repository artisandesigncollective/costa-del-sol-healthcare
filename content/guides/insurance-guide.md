# Private Health Insurance Spain - Complete Guide

## Introduction
Private health insurance is essential for most expats moving to Spain. This guide covers everything you need to know about choosing, buying, and using private health insurance in Spain.

## Do You Need Private Insurance?

### For Residency Applications
**Non-EU Citizens:**
- Private insurance is typically required for visa applications
- Must cover full period of initial visa (usually 1 year)
- Cannot have co-payments (no excess)
- Must be with a Spanish-registered insurer or international equivalent

**EU Citizens:**
- Can use EHIC/GHIC temporarily
- Recommended to get private insurance for comprehensive coverage

### Once You're a Resident
Once you're registered as a resident and paying into social security (through employment or self-employment), you qualify for Spain's public healthcare system. However, many expats keep private insurance for:

- Faster access to specialists
- English-speaking doctors
- Choice of doctor and hospital
- Private rooms
- Coverage outside Spain (with international plans)

## Major Spanish Insurance Providers

### Sanitas
**Best for:** Widest network, English-speaking support
- **Network:** 40,000+ professionals, 1,200+ centers
- **Costa del Sol coverage:** Excellent
- **English support:** Yes, dedicated international line
- **Typical cost:** €50-200/month depending on age

**Plans:**
- Sanitas Blua: Basic coverage
- Sanitas Mas Salud: Comprehensive
- Sanitas Premium: Top-tier with international coverage

### Adeslas
**Best for:** Competitive pricing
- **Network:** 40,000+ professionals
- **Costa del Sol coverage:** Very good
- **English support:** Limited
- **Typical cost:** €45-180/month

**Plans:**
- Adeslas Plena: Entry-level
- Adeslas Total: Comprehensive
- Adeslas Senior: For over 65s

### DKV
**Best for:** Premium service
- **Network:** 40,000+ professionals
- **Costa del Sol coverage:** Excellent
- **English support:** Yes
- **Typical cost:** €60-250/month

**Plans:**
- DKV Integral: Comprehensive
- DKV Mundisalud: With international coverage

### Cigna (International)
**Best for:** Frequent travelers
- **Network:** Global
- **Costa del Sol coverage:** Good (uses local networks)
- **English support:** Excellent
- **Typical cost:** €100-400/month

## International vs Spanish Insurance

| Feature | Spanish Insurance | International Insurance |
|---------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Cost | Lower | Higher |
| Local network | Extensive | Limited |
| English support | Varies | Usually excellent |
| Worldwide coverage | Optional add-on | Usually included |
| Pre-existing conditions | May be excluded | May be covered |
| Claims process | Simple | Can be complex |
| Direct billing | Very common | Varies |

## What to Look For

### Essential Coverage
- Hospitalization (full coverage, no limits)
- Surgery
- Specialist consultations
- Emergency care
- Diagnostic tests
- Cancer treatment

### Recommended Add-ons
- Dental care
- Optical care
- Physiotherapy
- Mental health
- Maternity (if planning children)

### Red Flags
- High co-payments/co-insurance
- Excessive waiting periods
- Low annual limits
- Exclusions for common conditions
- Poor customer reviews

## How to Apply

### Required Documents
- Passport or NIE
- Proof of address in Spain
- Bank account details (Spanish or international)
- Medical questionnaire (may require doctor's report for over 65s)

### Application Process
1. Get quotes from 3+ providers
2. Compare coverage carefully
3. Complete medical questionnaire
4. Submit application online or through broker
5. Receive policy documents (usually within 5-10 days)

## Using Your Insurance

### Finding Providers
- Check insurer's online directory
- Call insurer's helpline
- Ask for English-speaking doctors
- Verify coverage before treatment

### Direct Billing (Reembolso vs Cuadro Médico)
- **Cuadro Médico:** Use insurer's network, no upfront payment
- **Reembolso:** See any doctor, pay upfront, claim reimbursement

### Making Claims
- Keep all receipts and medical reports
- Submit claims within time limits (usually 6-12 months)
- Online portals make claiming easier
- Reimbursement typically takes 2-4 weeks

## Switching Insurance

You can change insurers, but be aware:
- New waiting periods may apply
- Pre-existing conditions may not be covered
- Always maintain continuous coverage
- Best to switch at renewal time

## FAQs

**Can I use my UK health insurance in Spain?**
- Some UK insurers offer European coverage
- Check if they have direct billing arrangements in Spain
- May need to pay upfront and claim back

**What about the S1 form for UK pensioners?**
- UK state pensioners can get an S1 form
- This gives access to Spanish public healthcare
- Can supplement with private insurance for faster access

**Is dental covered?**
- Usually as an add-on or separate policy
- Spanish dental plans are affordable (€10-30/month)
- Many dental clinics offer their own membership plans

**What about pre-existing conditions?**
- May be excluded or have waiting periods
- Be honest on application - claims can be denied for non-disclosure
- Some insurers specialize in covering pre-existing conditions
